Paella

Return to recipes

Description

Originally from the region of Valencia in Spain, this dish takes its name from the pan in which it is cooked. Beware, this recipe may not look like the photo.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 6 chicken thighs, skin off and roughly chopped
  • 100g chorizo, roughly chopped
  • 1 onion, roughly chopped
  • 6 garlic cloves, roughly chopped
  • 400g chopped tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon smoked paprika
  • 1.5 litres chicken stock
  • 250g bomba paella rice
  • 100g runner beans, trimmed and sliced
  • 100g butter beans
  • 200g broad beans
  • salt and ground black pepper

Steps

Adapted from a recipe by Matt Tebbutt.

  1. Heat oil in a large paella pan, casserole or frying pan
  2. Season the chicken thighs and cook until golden. Remove from pan and set aside
  3. Cook chorizo, onion, garlic, chopped tomatoes and smoked paprika to the pan for 3 minutes
  4. Return the chicken to the pan and add the rice. Stir until fully mixed and the rice is coated with oil
  5. Add the chicken stock and all of the beans to the pan, stir until ingredients are evenly distributed
  6. Bring to a gentle simmer and leave on low heat for 25 minutes or until the rice has absorbed the liquid
